# Downloaded Papers

1. [Rainbow Connection of Random Regular Graphs](1311.2299_dudek_frieze_tsourakakis_rainbow_regular.pdf)
   - Authors: Andrzej Dudek, Alan Frieze, Charalampos E. Tsourakakis
   - Year: 2014
   - arXiv: 1311.2299
   - Why relevant: PRIMARY USER-SPECIFIED PAPER. Proves rc(G(n,r)) = O(log n) for r ≥ 4 w.h.p. (best possible up to constant). Contains the main proof techniques: random greedy coloring, BFS tree expansion, Lemma on d-ary tree rainbow matchings.

2. [Rainbow Connection of Sparse Random Graphs](1201.4603_frieze_tsourakakis_rainbow_sparse_random.pdf)
   - Authors: Alan Frieze, Charalampos E. Tsourakakis
   - Year: 2012
   - arXiv: 1201.4603
   - Why relevant: PRIMARY USER-SPECIFIED PAPER. First paper to study rc of random regular graphs. Proves rc(G(n,r)) = O(log^4 n) for r = 3, O(log^{2θ_r} n) for r ≥ 4. Also proves rc(G(n,p)) ~ max{Z₁, diam} at the connectivity threshold.

3. [Some Remarks on Rainbow Connectivity](1501.00821_kamcev_krivelevich_sudakov_rainbow_remarks.pdf)
   - Authors: Nina Kamcev, Michael Krivelevich, Benny Sudakov
   - Year: 2015
   - arXiv: 1501.00821
   - Why relevant: Contains the best known upper bound: rc(G_{n,r}) ≤ c·log n / log r for r ≥ 5 (asymptotically tight up to constant). Key technique: Edge-Splitting Lemma. Also proves results for expanders.

4. [Rainbow Connection Number and Connected Dominating Sets](1010.2296_chandran_das_rainbow_domsets.pdf)
   - Authors: L. Sunil Chandran, Anita Das, Deepak Rajendraprasad, Nithin M. Varma
   - Year: 2010
   - arXiv: 1010.2296
   - Why relevant: Proves rc(G) ≤ 3n/(δ+1) + 3 for graphs with min degree δ (essentially optimal general bound). Shows rc(G) ≤ γ_c(G) + 2. Important for understanding the general vs. random graph distinction.

5. [Hardness and Algorithms for Rainbow Connection](0809.2493_chakraborty_rainbow_hardness.pdf)
   - Authors: Sourav Chakraborty, Eldar Fischer, Arie Matsliah, Raphael Yuster
   - Year: 2009
   - arXiv: 0809.2493
   - Why relevant: Proves computing rc(G) is NP-Hard (NP-Complete to decide rc(G) = 2). Also shows: connected graphs with min degree ≥ εn have bounded rc.

6. [On the Threshold for Rainbow Connection Number r in Random Graphs](1307.7747_heckel_riordan_rainbow_threshold.pdf)
   - Authors: Annika Heckel, Oliver Riordan
   - Year: 2013
   - arXiv: 1307.7747
   - Why relevant: Sharp threshold results for rc(G(n,p)) ≤ r (fixed r). Relevant for understanding threshold behavior and connections between rc and diameter.

7. [Rainbow Hamilton Cycles in Random Regular Graphs](math_0508145_janson_wormald_rainbow_hamilton.pdf)
   - Authors: Svante Janson, Nicholas Wormald
   - Year: 2005
   - arXiv: math/0508145
   - Why relevant: Uses contiguity of random regular graph models (foundational for Kamcev et al. approach).

8. [On Rainbow Connection Number and Connectivity](1105.5704_chandran_mathew_rainbow_connectivity.pdf)
   - Authors: L. Sunil Chandran, Rogers Mathew, Deepak Rajendraprasad
   - Year: 2011
   - arXiv: 1105.5704
   - Why relevant: Studies relationship between rc(G) and vertex-connectivity.

9. [On Rainbow Thresholds](2310.03974_han_yuan_rainbow_thresholds.pdf)
   - Authors: Jie Han, Xiaofan Yuan
   - Year: 2024
   - arXiv: 2310.03974
   - Why relevant: Recent work on rainbow thresholds using Frankston-Kahn-Narayanan-Park framework. Extends threshold theory to rainbow structures.
